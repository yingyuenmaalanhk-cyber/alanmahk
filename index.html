Biography

I am Ma Ying Yuen, a Form 6D student at S.K.H. Bishop Baker Secondary School. Over the years, I have grown from a reserved student into a proactive leader. I am passionate about STEM fields, especially Spatial Data Science, Artificial Intelligence, and Engineering Technology. Academically, I excel in mathematics, computer, and technology-related subjects, and have won awards in various competitions, including First Runner-up in PolyU's 'Build a Smart City Competition 2025' and Certificate of Merit in CUHK AI Hackathon 2025.

I am resilient, meticulous, and helpful, enjoy collaborating in teams to complete tasks, and am always willing to challenge myself and step out of my comfort zone. I have served in multiple leadership roles including Vice-head Prefect, which has developed my organizational and communication skills. I believe resilience and discipline are key to success, and I always maintain a growth mindset, viewing every challenge as an opportunity to improve. In the future, I hope to further my studies in engineering and become an engineer who contributes to society.

Personal Growth Journey

As a Hong Kong secondary school student, I am deeply passionate about engineering and technology. This passion led me to find a platform for practice in the MiTeen Club, where I have taken on important roles.

During my time in the MiTeen Society, I was responsible for coordinating and organizing STEAM lectures, workshops, and technology competitions both within and outside the school. These experiences not only enhanced my organizational skills but also deepened my understanding of the value of technology education. More importantly, I led teams to develop and implement multiple innovative projects, including a Smart Treadmill, an Automatic Claw Machine, and a Basketball Scoring Machine.

In these projects, I was fully involved in every stage from conception, design, assembly to debugging, coordinating team division of labor and integrating technical resources. These practical experiences have made me deeply appreciate the importance of combining theory with practice, and have helped the society develop a 'theoretical learning + practical innovation' development model. As mentioned in the principal's recommendation, the empathy, patience, and collaborative spirit I have demonstrated have earned the trust of teachers and peers, and these qualities have been fully reflected in team projects.
